Business Goal & Outcome
Shift listening from a functional activity to an identity-driven journey to drive sustained retention.
Amazon Music aims to improve retention across its tiers by creating a differentiated experience that becomes essential to users’ daily lives.
We reposition listening as an identity-driven journey based on Hook Model, aligning discovery, insight, and community with Amazon Music’s tier structure to drive retention and upgrades.
What did our process look like?
Research Methodology
We combined qualitative depth, quantitative scale, and behavioral evidence to understand retention challenges and engagement patterns.
User Behavior & Churn Insights
Bundled but unloved, Amazon Music carries for in-depth artist discovery yet lacks emotional pull and identity.
Across the market, users favor artist-led discovery, mood-based playlists, and socially influenced listening. While Amazon Music offers features like X-Ray that enable deeper storytelling, usage is still driven mainly by Prime bundling and device integration rather than intrinsic platform value.
As for Amazon Music, casual users stay for convenience, while passionate fans lack a sense of community and identity. As a result, the 30-day trial often fails to convert contemporary attention into long-term engagement.
A tiered experience that builds emotional connection is therefore needed.

Competitive Analysis
Its device-led advantage and untapped fan storytelling potential create a clear breakthrough opportunity.
This comparison shows how major music platforms differentiate, highlighting Amazon Music’s strength in device integration and its opportunity to expand fan interaction and deepen artist storytelling through X-Ray.
Brainstorming + Prioritization
Build reliability first, then amplify emotional value.
Reliability and usability set the baseline, but emotional connection drives true differentiation and long-term retention. Our strategy secures a seamless core experience first, then amplifies emotional value, executed in two key steps:
Step 1: Brainstormed and consolidated all ideas into a structured spreadsheet.
Step 2: Prioritized cost-effective ideas by balancing short-term impact with long-term emotional value.

Reflection
Differentiation Is Key
In a crowded market, standing out matters more than adding features. Early on, we struggled to find direction since most music platforms feel functionally similar. With guidance from Se One Park, Sanae Smili, and Jaden Young, we realized the redesign should amplify Amazon Music’s existing strengths rather than mimic competitors, making its unique ecosystem advantages clear, emotional, and hard to replace.
