Business Goal & Outcome
Supporting 10× growth requires a scalable, automated lead conversion system.
Founded in 2023, XDF Cultural Tourism is a new XDF business group backed by RMB 1 billion.
As travel services typically involve a long decision-making process, the business adopts a trust-based, content-driven lead conversion model, using social media content to attract and nurture leads before conversion process.
With the MVP flow validated and summer traffic expected to surge, the business plans to scale volume by 10×. This growth makes a streamlined, automation-driven sales workflow essential, as lead intake, distribution, and follow-up are currently manual amid high sales turnover and training costs.

Issue #1
Manual calling significantly increases sales effort while offering low pickup success.
WeChat friend approvals currently rely on phone calls, which require significant sales effort yet yield low pickup rates, often over 40 calls for a single approval. In China, calls from unknown numbers are widely perceived as intrusive or spam, leading most consumers to ignore them and further reducing efficiency.
Decision #1
Efficiency
A progressive outreach flow powered by automated SMS and calls, improving both efficiency and transparency.
Calls are handled through a third-party outbound platform integrated with the CRM, enabling one-click dialing. SMS was added as a low-interruption pre-step, using templated messages with dynamic details and a digital business card to build credibility before calls.
Issue #2
Follow-ups are fragmented and hard to act on.
When revisiting follow-ups or taking over leads, sales must review history. However, the current process requires 3 steps, and the contact page lacks clear hierarchy, making key information hard to find.

Sales Process Transparency
Rebuilding lead management around context, action, and efficiency.
The redesign shifts from a table-first layout to a context-preserving, action-oriented workflow. The lead list acts as a control center with a simplified toolbar surfacing only decision-critical controls. Table and board views support different sales workflows, while side panels display contact details and follow-up timelines without breaking context.
Information is grouped by intent to reduce noise, and integrated SMS and calls allow AI to summarize interactions and suggest next steps. New traveler details are captured through a one-column modal, minimizing eye movement and isolating the task to ensure a clear start-to-finish flow.
Issue #3
Information is present, but action is not enabled.
The homepage surfaces data, but not decisions. It increases reading effort instead of guiding action.

Reflection
Context Continuity, Information Hierarchy, and Action-Oriented Design
This project demonstrated how quickly efficiency deteriorates when context is lost. In the end, what at first seemed to be isolated UXUI problems were signs of a more serious structural issue with workflow design and information architecture, like fragmented follow-ups, cluttered pages, and manual labor. By reframing lead management around context continuity, action proximity, and progressive guidance, I learned that effective UX is less about adding features and more about removing friction between intention and execution. The result reinforced my conviction that effective enterprise UX should subtly direct users toward the next appropriate course of action without requiring additional focus or effort.
